Slaves of Fate

.01 | AN INTRODUCTION
Mostafa El Beheiry

Fate truly is that which spurs on the world. It is said that a book is kept above the lands of men, above the world itself with all its kingdoms, realms and plains. A book of fate, a book in which history itself is written, it is also said that the last few pages of this book remain unwritten.

Kings, queens, lords and ladies, adventurers and bounty hunters all alike sought this so called fabled book. “The Naruq” as men called it, a relic left behind of ages past, of civilizations left in ruin, and some in hiding.

It is ours that all men perish, even ourselves. Such is the way of The Guild

Prophecies were made, many they were, and false were most of them. Yet one prophecy seemed looming to hit the mark, and it was the most unlikely of them all. It spoke of isolated men, a people who had no claim in the world than for their kin’s blood. Men who could not possibly conceive what true power was, yet one day they would hold it.

The power to change the history of more than mankind, the power to send their world into glory, or plunge it in chaos.
